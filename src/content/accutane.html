What Is Accutane?

Isotretinoin is a prescription medication used to treat severe nodular acne that has not responded to other treatments, including antibiotics. It is taken by mouth in two divided doses with food. The dose is based on your body weight, and a typical course of treatment lasts about 4 to 5 months or longer depending on your response to treatment.

Isotretinoin (Accutane) works by:

1

Reduces oil production

Significantly reduces oil production in your skin

2

Decreases inflammation

Reduces redness and swelling associated with severe acne

3

Prevents clogged pores

Stops pores from becoming blocked with oil and dead skin cells

4

Reduces bacteria

Decreases acne-causing bacteria on the skin

Side Effects

** Side effects are dose-dependent and reversible after stopping the medication.

Common

1

Dry skin and lips

Apply a fragrance-free moisturizer frequently throughout the day. Use a thick lip balm or petroleum jelly (Vaseline®, Aquaphor®) on lips multiple times daily. Avoid hot showers and harsh soaps.

2

Dry eyes

Use preservative-free artificial tears throughout the day. Consider removing contact lenses if dryness becomes uncomfortable.

Less common but reported

1

Joint and muscle aches

May experience soreness during treatment

2

Mood changes

Report any significant mood changes to your provider

3

Nosebleeds

Due to dry nasal passages — use saline nasal spray

4

Sun sensitivity

Use sunscreen daily (SPF 30 or higher)

5

"Purging"

Temporary worsening of acne during the first few weeks of treatment

6

Lab changes

Elevated cholesterol, triglycerides, or liver enzymes (monitored with blood tests)

What To Expect During Treatment

1

Initial purging

Acne may temporarily worsen during the first 2–4 weeks — this is expected and not a reason to stop treatment

2

Improvement by 2–3 months

Most patients see noticeable improvement by this point

3

Long-term remission

Many patients experience long-term acne remission after completing a full course

4

Maintenance therapy

Topical therapy is often recommended after completion

5

Possible second course

8–20% of patients may require a second course if acne returns

While On Treatment, DO NOT

!

Donate blood

During treatment or for 1 month after stopping

!

Share your medication

Never share isotretinoin with anyone

!

Cosmetic procedures

No waxing, dermabrasion, or laser treatments during treatment or for 6 months after (risk of scarring)

!

Take vitamin A supplements

May cause additive toxic effects

!

Take tetracycline antibiotics

E.g., doxycycline — this combination can increase intracranial pressure

iPLEDGE Program Enrollment (Required)

All patients must be enrolled in the iPLEDGE Program, a mandatory FDA safety program designed to prevent pregnancy while taking isotretinoin.

Why is this important?

Isotretinoin causes severe birth defects if taken during pregnancy. The risk of miscarriage is about 20%, and the risk of birth defects is 18% to 28%.

1

Enrollment required

Must be enrolled before starting treatment and sign informed consent forms

2

Lab studies

FASTING laboratory studies (cholesterol, triglycerides, liver tests) must be completed within 5 days prior to next appointment, scheduled in 1 month

For Patients Who Can Become Pregnant:

1

Two pregnancy tests required

Before starting treatment. A negative test is REQUIRED on the day of enrollment.

2

Testing options

In-office pregnancy test ($15), OR home test — email photo to: marissacalderm@icloud.com (must include: date, Government ID, and negative result)

3

Second test with labs

Included with laboratory work completed within 5 days prior to your next appointment

4

Two forms of contraception

Begin 1 month before treatment, continue during, and continue for 1 month after stopping isotretinoin

Monthly Treatment Requirements (IMPORTANT)

1

Monthly visit required

Office visit or telehealth visit. In-office and virtual visits may alternate if scheduling is difficult.

2

Lab monitoring

Checked approximately every 3 months (may be more frequent if clinically indicated)

3

Pregnancy testing

Patients who can become pregnant: Monthly pregnancy testing is mandatory — must confirm NEGATIVE

Reminder

Isotretinoin is a highly effective medication when used correctly. Most side effects are temporary and manageable. Strict adherence to pregnancy prevention requirements, monthly visits, and laboratory monitoring is essential for safe and successful treatment. Please contact your healthcare provider with any questions or concerns during treatment.
